Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I do the exercises in the default routines?

    We’ve included instructions here for how to perform each of the six exercises included in the three default routines.

    For most people, though, the easiest way to learn exercises is by watching someone else perform them while talking through what they’re doing, so we’ve also included a link to a youtube search for each of the exercises. We’d encourage you to take a look at a few videos to get a better understanding of how to perform the exercises with the best form.

    Chin-ups

    Stand underneath the bar and grip it with your palms facing towards you and roughly shoulder width apart. Pull upwards with your arms until your head is above the bar. Hold for a moment, then lower yourself back down until your arms are straight. If your routine calls for more than one rep per set, don’t fully lockout your arms between the reps - keep a little bend in your elbows.

    Find videos of chin-up form.

    Push-ups

    Start facing the ground with your legs fully extended behind you, with your upper body supported by your arms. Your hands should be roughly shoulder width apart and there should be a slight bend in your elbows. Lower yourself towards the ground by bending your arms until your chest reaches the floor, then push back up until you return to the starting p osition. Your body should be as straight as possible from your shoulders down to your feet throughout the whole exercise, so keep your core muscles engaged.

    Find videos of push-up form.

    Squats

    Begin in a standing position with your feet shoulder-width apart. Lower your body by bending at the knees until your hips are around level with your knees, then push backup to the starting position.

    Find videos of squat-up form.

    Pull-ups

    Pull-ups are performed in the same way as chin-ups, but grip the bar with your palms facing away from your body. This puts more of an emphasis on the back and shoulder muscles.

    Find videos of pull-up form.

    Close grip push-ups

    Close grip push-ups are performed in the same way as standard push-ups, but with the hands placed closer together on the ground - roughly one hand width apart.

    Find videos of close grip push-up form.

    Lunges

    From a standing position, step forward with one leg and then bend both knees until the lower part of your back leg and the upper part of your front leg are almost parallel to the ground. Next, push back up to return to the starting position. Your upper body should be straight with your core engaged throughout the movement.

    Find videos of lunge form.
